A bit about me!

I am a current graduate student in the MIT-WHOI Joint Program in Oceanography/Applied Ocean Engineering and Sciences, working in the laboratory of Dr. Kassandra Costa on paleoceanography with a particular emphasis on the North Pacific.

I was born and raised on the 200 million year old Palisades Sill unit, in New York. I attended Barnard College of Columbia University, receiving my B.A. in Environmental Science (minors in chemistry, architecture) in 2023. I wrote my senior thesis work with Prof. Dr. Jerry McManus on constraining the movement of the carbonate compensation depth (CCD) in the Northeast Pacific over the past 800 kyrs.
